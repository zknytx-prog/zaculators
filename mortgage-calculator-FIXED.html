What Does This Calculator Do?

This mortgage calculator uses the standard loan amortization formula to calculate your monthly mortgage payment based on three key factors: the loan amount (home price minus down payment), the interest rate, and the loan term.

The calculator shows you:

  • ✓ Loan Amount: The amount you're borrowing (after down payment)
  • ✓ Monthly Payment: Principal + interest payment due each month
  • ✓ Total Interest: All interest you'll pay over the life of the loan
  • ✓ Total Amount Paid: Principal + all interest combined

These calculations help you understand the true cost of homeownership and make informed decisions about your mortgage.

Understanding Your Results

Here's what each result means and how to interpret it:

Loan Amount

This is the total amount you're borrowing from the lender. It equals the home price minus your down payment. For example, if you're buying a $300,000 home with a $60,000 down payment, your loan amount would be $240,000.

Monthly Payment

This is the amount you'll pay to your lender each month for the duration of your loan (15, 20, or 30 years). This payment includes both principal (the amount you borrowed) and interest (the cost of borrowing). Keep in mind this doesn't include property taxes, homeowners insurance, PMI, or HOA fees.

Total Interest Paid

This is the total amount of interest you'll pay over the entire life of the loan. It can be a surprisingly large number - often 50% or more of the loan amount on a 30-year mortgage. This is why paying extra principal early can save you significant money.

Total Amount Paid

This is the sum of everything you'll pay: the principal loan amount plus all interest. For example, on a $240,000 loan at 6.5% over 30 years, you'll pay about $475,000 total - $240,000 principal plus $235,000 interest.

Example: Comparing Loan Terms

Here's how a 30-year and 15-year mortgage compare on a $240,000 loan at 6.5% interest:

Loan Term Monthly Payment Total Interest Total Paid
15 years $1,854 $93,720 $333,720
30 years $1,520 $307,020 $547,020

As you can see, the 15-year mortgage has a higher monthly payment but saves you over $213,000 in interest! However, the 30-year mortgage is more affordable monthly, which is why most homeowners choose it.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does this calculator include property taxes and insurance? ▼

No, this calculator shows only the principal and interest portion of your monthly payment. Your actual monthly payment (PITI) also includes property taxes, homeowners insurance, and PMI (if applicable). On average, these add 25-35% to your monthly payment. Contact your lender for exact amounts based on your property and location.

What's the difference between 15, 20, and 30-year mortgages? ▼

15-year mortgage: Higher monthly payment, but you own your home faster and pay much less interest. Good if you want to build equity quickly.

30-year mortgage: Lower monthly payment, easier to afford, but you pay significantly more interest over time. Most common choice.

20-year mortgage: A middle ground between the two, offering a balance of affordability and total interest savings.

What's PMI and do I need it? ▼

PMI (Private Mortgage Insurance) is required when your down payment is less than 20%. It protects the lender if you default. For example, with a 10% down payment, you'd pay PMI until you reach 20% equity. PMI typically costs 0.3-1.5% of your loan amount annually and is added to your monthly payment.

How does making extra payments help? ▼

When you make extra principal payments, you reduce the amount you owe faster, which means less interest accrues over time. For example, making one extra mortgage payment per year can cut 5-7 years off your 30-year mortgage and save you tens of thousands in interest. Even small extra payments ($50-100/month) add up significantly over time.

What's a good interest rate? ▼

Interest rates vary based on market conditions, your credit score, loan type, and down payment. As of 2026, rates typically range from 5.5% to 8%. Shop around with multiple lenders - even 0.5% difference in interest rate can mean $50,000+ in savings over 30 years. Check current rates on Bankrate, LendingTree, or directly with lenders.

Is this calculator accurate? ▼

Yes, this calculator uses the standard amortization formula used by banks and lenders. However, it shows only principal and interest. For your actual monthly payment, you need to add property taxes, insurance, PMI, and HOA fees. Always verify with your lender before making financial decisions.
