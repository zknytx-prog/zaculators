What to do next

  1. Use consistent conditions if you repeat the test.
  2. Warm up properly before trying it.
  3. Do not sprint the first minute and implode later.
  4. Track trends over time rather than obsessing over one result.
  5. Use medical guidance if you have health concerns.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is VO2 max?

VO2 max is a measure of aerobic capacity and oxygen use during intense exercise.

Is the Cooper test exact?

No. It is a field estimate, not a lab test.

Why does pacing matter?

Poor pacing can lower the distance and skew the estimate.

Can beginners use this?

They can, but they should use caution and appropriate effort.

Should I compare my score across ages?

Age and sex affect interpretation, so general comparisons can be misleading.

Can I improve VO2 max?

Cardio training can improve it over time.
