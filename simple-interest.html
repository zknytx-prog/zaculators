Frequently Asked Questions

What is simple interest?

Simple interest is calculated only on the original principal, not on previously earned interest.

When is simple interest used?

It is common in basic loans, short-term agreements, and educational examples.

How is this different from compound interest?

Compound interest adds interest on top of interest, while simple interest stays tied to the original amount only.

Can I use partial years?

Yes. Decimal years are allowed, so 1.5 years works for 18 months.

Why is the total lower than compound interest?

Because simple interest does not reinvest the interest as time passes.

Is this a legal lending calculator?

No. It is a formula tool for estimation. Real contracts may use fees, compounding, and other terms.
