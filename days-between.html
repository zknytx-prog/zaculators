Frequently Asked Questions

Does this count the start date?

The result shows the elapsed time between the two selected calendar dates, which is the most common planning approach.

Can the end date be earlier than the start date?

No. The page blocks that so you do not accidentally reverse the dates and get a misleading result.

Why are months shown as approximate?

Month lengths vary, so the calculator estimates months from the exact day count for quick planning.

Is this useful for contracts or invoices?

Yes for estimating timing, but legal and billing rules may count days differently, so confirm the method required in your document.

Can I use this for project planning?

Yes. It is useful for timelines, notice periods, delivery windows, and milestone spacing.

Does leap year affect the output?

Yes. The exact day count automatically reflects leap years and month length changes.
