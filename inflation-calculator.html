Frequently Asked Questions

What does this inflation calculator show?

It estimates how much more money you may need in the future to buy what a current amount buys today.

Is inflation always the same every year?

No. This tool assumes a steady average rate, which is useful for planning but not a forecast of actual annual changes.

Why does a small rate matter over time?

Even modest inflation compounds, so the long-term effect can be much larger than people expect.

Can I use this for salary planning?

Yes. It can help you think about future budgets, raises, savings goals, and retirement targets.

Does this replace investment advice?

No. It only shows the effect of inflation, not taxes, investment returns, or risk.

What rate should I use?

Use a realistic long-run average for your region or the rate your own planner or institution uses.
