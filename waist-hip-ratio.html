What to do next

  1. Measure consistently using the same tape and method.
  2. Compare this with other health markers.
  3. Track trends over months, not days.
  4. Ask a clinician if you need personalized interpretation.
  5. Do not turn one ratio into a personality trait.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does waist-to-hip ratio show?

It is a body-shape screening measure related to fat distribution.

Is it enough by itself?

No. It should be interpreted along with other health information.

How should I measure waist?

Follow a consistent method, typically around the natural waist or just above the navel depending on the standard used.

Can I track this over time?

Yes. It is often more useful as a trend than a one-time reading.

Does a lower ratio always mean healthy?

Not automatically. Health is more complicated than one ratio.

Why not use BMI only?

Because body size and fat distribution are different things.
