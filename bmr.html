What to do next

  1. Use BMR as a base, not your final calorie target.
  2. Add activity with a TDEE calculator for a more practical estimate.
  3. Recalculate after major weight changes.
  4. Avoid extreme calorie deficits without supervision.
  5. Use trends in energy, weight, and performance to adjust.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is BMR?

BMR is the energy your body uses at rest for basic functions like breathing and circulation.

Is BMR the same as TDEE?

No. TDEE adds activity on top of resting needs.

Why estimate it?

It helps create a starting point for calorie planning.

Is this exact?

No. It is an estimate, not a lab measurement.

Should I eat exactly my BMR?

Usually not. Most people need more than BMR because daily movement adds demand.

Can body composition affect the estimate?

Yes. Lean mass differences can change real-world calorie needs.
