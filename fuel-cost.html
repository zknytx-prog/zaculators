What to do next

  1. Use your real-world MPG from recent fill-ups instead of brochure numbers.
  2. Compare fuel costs across two route options.
  3. Add tolls and parking for a complete trip budget.
  4. Re-check gas prices before a long trip because they move around constantly.
  5. Use the result to decide whether carpooling makes sense.

Frequently Asked Questions

Should I use highway or mixed MPG?

Use the most realistic MPG for your actual driving pattern, not the best-case sticker number.

Can I use diesel prices here?

Yes. The math works the same as long as the price unit matches your fuel economy unit.

Why is cost per mile useful?

It helps compare routes, estimate delivery expenses, and set travel reimbursement rates.

Does traffic change the result?

Yes. Stop-and-go traffic usually lowers MPG and increases total fuel cost.

Should I round up gallons needed?

For planning, yes. It gives a safer budget cushion.

Can I estimate a round trip?

Yes. Enter the full round-trip miles instead of one-way distance.
