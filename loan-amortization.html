Frequently Asked Questions

What is an amortization schedule?

It shows how each payment is split between principal and interest over the life of the loan.

Why is the first payment mostly interest?

At the start, the balance is highest, so the interest portion is larger and the principal portion is smaller.

Does this work for zero-interest loans?

Yes. In that case the payment is simply the loan amount divided evenly by the number of months.

Why only show the first 12 months?

That preview is enough for quick checking and prints cleanly, without turning the page into a spreadsheet graveyard.

Can I use this for mortgages and car loans?

Yes for estimation. Real lenders may add insurance, escrow, fees, or different compounding rules.

What changes the total interest most?

The rate and term length usually matter the most. Longer loans often lower the monthly payment but raise total interest.
