What to do next

  1. Use realistic MET values, not fantasy numbers.

  4. Use this with your overall calorie plan.
  5. Adjust based on real progress and recovery.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a MET value?

A MET is a standard way to describe activity intensity relative to rest.

Why is this only an estimate?

Real burn varies with technique, effort, fitness, and environment.

Can I use this for walking?

Yes, if you know the approximate MET value for your pace.

Does body weight matter?

Yes. Heavier bodies generally burn more energy for the same task.

Is smartwatch data better?

Sometimes, but even wearables are still estimates.

Why compare hourly burn?

It helps compare activities of different durations.
