Frequently Asked Questions

What does EMI mean?

EMI stands for Equated Monthly Installment, the fixed payment amount due each month on many installment loans.

Is EMI the same as amortized payment?

For standard fixed-rate installment loans, yes, the monthly payment is calculated using the same core formula.

Can the EMI stay fixed while interest changes?

Not on a true fixed-rate loan. Variable-rate loans can change the payment or the loan term.

Why is the total repayment much higher than the loan amount?

Because the total includes all the interest paid over the life of the loan.

Does this include fees or insurance?

No. It shows the base loan payment only.

Can I use this for personal, education, or vehicle loans?

Yes for estimation, provided the loan uses fixed monthly installment math.
