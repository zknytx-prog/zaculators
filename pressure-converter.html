Frequently Asked Questions

What is PSI used for?

PSI is common in tires, compressors, plumbing, and many U.S. consumer pressure references.

Why do science references use pascals?

The pascal is the SI pressure unit, so it is standard in physics, engineering, and technical documents.

Is bar close to atmosphere pressure?

One atmosphere is slightly above one bar, which is why the numbers often look close but are not identical.

Can I use this for tire pressure?

Yes for unit conversion. Always follow the vehicle sticker or manufacturer guidance for the target pressure.

Why does exact pressure matter?

Small errors can affect safety, performance, calibration, and process control.

Should I round pressure readings?

Round only after conversion, and use the level of precision required by the tool or task.
