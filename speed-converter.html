Frequently Asked Questions

What is a knot?

A knot is one nautical mile per hour. It is widely used in aviation and marine navigation.

Why are meters per second useful?

Meters per second are common in science, physics, and engineering calculations.

When should I use km/h instead of mph?

km/h is standard in most countries, while mph remains common in the United States and a few others.

Can I use this for running pace?

Not directly. Running pace is typically minutes per mile or kilometer, which is the inverse style of speed.

Does wind speed use the same conversion?

Yes. The unit math is the same whether the value describes a car, a boat, or the wind.

Why convert all units at once?

It helps when specs, signs, GPS systems, and technical documents all use different speed units.
