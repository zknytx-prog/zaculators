Frequently Asked Questions

What's a good cash-on-cash return?

8-12% annually is solid. Below 5% may not justify risk. Above 15% often indicates underpriced property or high risk. Compare against stock market returns (~10% historical average). Real estate gives tax benefits (depreciation deduction) stocks don't have.

What's cap rate vs cash-on-cash return?

Cap Rate = (Annual Net Income / Purchase Price). Cash-on-Cash = (Annual Net Income / Cash Invested). Cap rate ignores financing; cash-on-cash shows actual return on your money. Both matter for investment decisions.

How do I estimate repair costs?

Industry standard: 1% of property value annually. For $300K property = $3K/year maintenance budget. Varies by age/condition. Older properties cost more. Budget higher for first investment (learning curve, surprises). Keep reserves.

Should I account for appreciation?

No, for conservative analysis. Properties typically appreciate 2-3% annually long-term, but shouldn't count on it. Your profit comes from cash flow + tax benefits. Appreciation is upside bonus. This calculator focuses on current cash flow (real earnings).

What about taxes on rental income?

Rental income is taxable, but deductible expenses reduce taxable amount. Depreciation deduction (non-cash) saves significant taxes. Consult tax professional for your situation. Entity structure (LLC, S-Corp) affects taxes. Proper structure saves 20-30% taxes.

Is this property cash-flowing?

Positive cash flow: Monthly rent > all expenses. Zero cash flow: Breaks even (appreciate only). Negative cash flow: Lose money monthly (risky). For investment property, aim for positive cash flow minimum. Negative works only if strong appreciation expected (risky).
