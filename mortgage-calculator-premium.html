Common Questions About Mortgages

PMI (Private Mortgage Insurance) is required when your down payment is less than 20% of the home price. It protects the lender in case you default. PMI typically costs 0.5â€“1% of your loan amount annually. Once you reach 20% equity through payments or appreciation, you can request it be removed.

Mortgage interest is calculated monthly based on your remaining loan balance and your annual interest rate. Early in the loan, most of your payment covers interest. Over time, as principal decreases, more of each payment goes toward building equity. This is why a 30-year mortgage costs significantly more total interest than a 15-year loan.
