What This Calculator Estimates

This self-employment tax calculator estimates self-employment tax from net self-employment income and the tax rate you enter. It is useful for first-pass planning when setting aside money for quarterly payments or checking how income changes affect your estimate.

How to Use This Calculator

  1. Enter estimated net self-employment income after business expenses.
  2. Enter the self-employment tax rate you want to test.
  3. Click Estimate Tax to calculate the tax amount.
  4. Review the amount left after that tax in the detail line.
  5. Recalculate if your income or assumed rate changes.

Formula / Method Used

  • Estimated self-employment tax = Net self-employment income x Tax rate
  • Estimated income after self-employment tax = Net self-employment income - Estimated self-employment tax

Worked Example

If net self-employment income is $80,000 and the tax rate is 15.3%, the estimated self-employment tax is $12,240. The amount left after that tax is about $67,760.

What the Result Means

The result is the estimated self-employment tax based on the simple rate you entered. The detail line shows the estimated amount left after that tax, which can help with cash-flow planning.

Common Mistakes

  • Entering gross revenue instead of net self-employment income.
  • Assuming self-employment tax is the same as total tax due.
  • Ignoring thresholds, caps, deductions, or deductible portions of the tax.
  • Using last year's rate assumptions after rules change.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does this self-employment tax calculator estimate?

It estimates self-employment tax by applying the tax rate you enter to net self-employment income, then shows the remaining amount after that tax.

Is this the same as total income tax?

No. This page estimates self-employment tax only and does not automatically include income tax, deductions, credits, or local taxes.

What income should I enter?

Use estimated net self-employment income after business expenses, not total gross revenue.

Why can the final tax be different?

Final tax can differ because official rules can include wage limits, deductible portions, other income, filing status, and country-specific thresholds.

Where should I verify current rules?

Verify current rates with the official government source before relying on the estimate for a final filing or quarterly tax decision.

General Disclaimer

This calculator provides self-employment tax estimates only and is not tax, legal, payroll, or accounting advice. Final obligations depend on current law, other income, deductions, and filing details.
