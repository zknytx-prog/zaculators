Payroll Calculator

What This Calculator Estimates

This calculator estimates gross pay from hourly rate and hours worked, then applies an estimated withholding percentage to show approximate net (take-home) pay for the period.

Formula / Method Used

Gross Pay = Hourly Rate × Hours Worked. Withholding Amount = Gross Pay × (Withholding % ÷ 100). Net Pay = Gross Pay − Withholding Amount.

Worked Example

At $25/hour for 80 hours, gross pay is $2,000. At 25% estimated withholding, that's $500 withheld, leaving an estimated net pay of $1,500.

How to Interpret the Result

This is a rough estimate for planning purposes. Actual withholding depends on filing status, state, deductions, and benefits — use certified payroll software for exact paycheck amounts.

Common Mistakes

  • Using a flat withholding percentage without accounting for progressive tax brackets.
  • Forgetting state or local income tax where applicable.
  • Treating this estimate as a substitute for an actual pay stub.
  • Not accounting for pre-tax deductions like 401(k) or health insurance.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does this payroll calculator estimate?

It estimates an employee's gross pay and an approximate net pay after standard tax withholding percentages.

What withholding rate should I use?

Use a combined estimate of federal, state, and payroll tax withholding relevant to the employee, often 20-30% for many US employees.

Does this replace an actual payroll system?

No, this gives a quick estimate only. Use certified payroll software or a professional for actual paycheck processing and tax filings.

Is this for hourly or salaried employees?

It works for both — enter total gross pay for the period regardless of how it was calculated.

Does this include employer-side payroll taxes?

No, this estimates employee take-home pay only. See the Employee Cost Calculator for total employer cost including employer-side taxes.
