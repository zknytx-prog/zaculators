Markup Calculator

What This Calculator Estimates

This calculator estimates your selling price and profit per unit based on your cost and a target markup percentage, and shows the resulting margin for comparison.

Formula / Method Used

Selling Price = Cost × (1 + Markup % ÷ 100). Profit = Selling Price − Cost. Resulting Margin % = (Profit ÷ Selling Price) × 100.

Worked Example

A $20 cost with a 50% markup gives a $30 selling price, $10 profit per unit, and a resulting margin of 33.3% — not 50%, since markup and margin are calculated differently.

How to Interpret the Result

Use the selling price to set your listed price, and the margin figure to understand your actual profitability rate — the two numbers will always differ except at 0%.

Common Mistakes

  • Confusing markup percentage with margin percentage when pricing.
  • Forgetting to include shipping, packaging, or platform fees in "cost."
  • Using the same markup across products with very different cost structures.
  • Not revisiting markup as supplier costs change.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does this markup calculator estimate?

It estimates your selling price and profit per unit based on your cost and desired markup percentage.

Is markup the same as margin?

No. Markup is calculated on cost, while margin is calculated on selling price — a 50% markup is not the same as a 50% margin.

How do I convert markup to margin?

Margin % = Markup % ÷ (100% + Markup %) × 100. This calculator shows both automatically.

What markup percentage should I use?

It depends on your industry, competition, and target profit — retail often uses 50-100% markup, wholesale often less.

Does this include taxes or fees?

No, this calculates markup on cost only. Add taxes or transaction fees separately if relevant to your pricing.
