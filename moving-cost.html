What Is the Moving Cost Calculator?

The Moving Cost Calculator estimates how much you can expect to spend when relocating — whether across town or across the country. Moving costs vary enormously based on distance, volume of belongings, labor rates in your area, packing services, storage needs, and timing. According to American Moving and Storage Association data, the average cost of a local move (under 100 miles) runs $1,250–$2,500, while interstate moves average $4,300–$9,000 depending on distance and weight. People who underestimate moving costs often face budget shortfalls that compound the stress of an already demanding life event. This calculator pulls together the key variables — home size, distance, service level, and add-ons — to give you a realistic number before you request a single quote.

How to Use This Calculator

  1. Current Home Size — Enter the number of bedrooms or approximate square footage. A studio move costs far less than a 4-bedroom household with full garage and attic.
  2. Move Distance — Local, intrastate, or interstate. Crossing state lines triggers federal DOT regulations and typically increases cost significantly.
  3. Service Type — Self-move (truck rental), partial service (movers load/unload), or full service (packing, transport, unpacking). Full service can cost 3–4x a DIY move.
  4. Packing Materials — Boxes, tape, bubble wrap, and specialty containers for art or electronics. Budget $100–$400 for a 2-bedroom move.
  5. Storage Needs — If there is a gap between move-out and move-in, add storage unit costs ($100–$300/month for a 10x10 unit).
  6. Additional Services — Piano or specialty item moving, vehicle transport, debris disposal, or cleaning services.

Understanding Your Results

Your estimated moving cost is a starting range — actual quotes from movers may be higher or lower depending on local labor markets, fuel surcharges, and seasonal demand. Moving companies charge peak-season rates (May through September, end of month) that run 20–30% higher than off-peak. Always get at least three binding estimates rather than non-binding quotes, which can increase at delivery. The estimate does not include often-overlooked costs: utility deposits at the new location, new furnishings that don't fit the new space, restaurant meals during the chaotic first week, and potential repairs to the old property before returning a security deposit. Build a 15–20% buffer into your moving budget.

Example Calculation

A family moves a 3-bedroom house 800 miles from Chicago to Dallas in mid-June with full packing service. Movers quote $6,800 for transport based on an estimated 7,500 lb shipment. Packing service adds $650. The family needs a 10x10 storage unit for 3 weeks at $180. Packing materials purchased separately cost $220. Vehicle transport adds $900. Total cost: $8,750. Had they chosen a DIY truck rental (Budget or Penske), the truck alone for an 800-mile one-way move would cost approximately $1,200–$1,800, plus fuel ($300–$500), hotels en route ($150–$200), and hired help at both ends ($400–$600) — a DIY total of roughly $2,500, saving over $6,000 but requiring 2–3 days of driving and labor.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the cheapest time of year to move?

Moving costs are lowest from October through April, particularly mid-week and mid-month. Demand drops sharply outside of summer and end-of-month periods when leases typically turn over. Moving on a Tuesday or Wednesday rather than a Friday or Saturday can save 15–25% on labor costs alone. If your timeline is flexible, targeting a January or February move can cut full-service costs by $500–$1,500 compared to peak summer rates.

What is a binding estimate vs. a non-binding estimate?

A binding estimate is a guaranteed price — the mover cannot charge more than quoted unless you add services. A non-binding estimate is just a prediction; the final bill can be higher based on actual weight or time. Federal law requires movers to provide written estimates, and for interstate moves, they must release your belongings upon payment of no more than 110% of a non-binding estimate. Always request binding or binding-not-to-exceed estimates for budget certainty.

Are moving expenses tax deductible?

As of the 2017 Tax Cuts and Jobs Act, moving expenses are no longer deductible for most civilians. The deduction was suspended through 2025. Active-duty military members who move due to a permanent change of station order remain eligible. If you are relocating for work and your employer reimburses moving costs, those reimbursements are generally treated as taxable wages. Check with a tax professional for your specific situation, particularly if you are self-employed.

How do I protect my belongings during a move?

Interstate movers are required to offer basic Released Value Protection (60 cents per pound per item) at no charge — but this is minimal. Full Value Protection, which replaces or repairs damaged items at current market value, costs extra (typically $200–$600 for a standard move). Your homeowner's or renter's insurance may cover moves — check your policy. For high-value items like art, jewelry, or electronics, consider a standalone transit insurance policy from a specialty insurer.

How do I avoid moving scams?

Rogue movers are a real problem — the FMCSA receives thousands of complaints annually. Verify any interstate mover's USDOT number on the FMCSA website before signing anything. Red flags include demanding large cash deposits upfront, providing a quote without seeing your belongings, operating without a physical address, or quoting prices far below competitors. Use the FMCSA's mover search tool, check BBB ratings, and read reviews on Google and Yelp before committing.
