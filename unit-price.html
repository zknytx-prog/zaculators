Frequently Asked Questions

What is unit price?

It is the cost per one unit of quantity, such as per ounce, pound, liter, item, or sheet.

Why compare by unit price instead of total price?

A larger package can cost more overall while still being the better deal per unit.

Can I use weight, volume, or count as the quantity?

Yes. The math works as long as both products use the same quantity basis.

What happens if the package sizes use different units?

Convert them first so both quantities use the same unit before comparing.

Does a lower unit price always mean I should buy it?

Not always. Expiration risk, storage space, and quality still matter.

Why is this useful for business buying?

It helps with purchasing, supplier comparison, menu costing, and inventory decisions.
