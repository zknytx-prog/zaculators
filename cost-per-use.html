Frequently Asked Questions

What does cost per use tell me?

It spreads the purchase cost across how often you realistically expect to use the item, which helps compare value instead of just sticker price.

Why is this useful for business buying?

It helps you compare durable tools, software seats, appliances, and equipment on a practical cost basis.

What if I overestimate the lifetime uses?

Your cost per use will look too low, so it is better to use a realistic or slightly conservative estimate.

Can I compare expensive and cheap versions of the same product?

Yes. A higher purchase price can still win if the item lasts longer or gets used more often.

Does this include maintenance or replacement parts?

No. This version focuses on purchase price only, so total ownership cost may be higher.

Is monthly value consumed the same as depreciation?

Not exactly. It is just a planning estimate based on your expected use pattern.
