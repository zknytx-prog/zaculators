What Is the Grade Converter?

A grade converter translates a numerical percentage score into a letter grade or GPA equivalent according to standard academic grading scales. Students use it when applying to graduate schools that require a GPA conversion, when transferring between institutions with different scales, or simply to understand what a score like 87% means in letter-grade terms. Teachers and counselors use converters to standardize grades across courses graded differently. Because grading scales vary by country, institution, and even department, a reliable converter clarifies exactly where a score falls on the most common frameworks.

How to Use This Calculator

  1. Percentage score — Enter your numerical grade as a percentage between 0 and 100.
  2. Grading scale — Use the standard US letter grade or 4.0 GPA mapping that applies to your institution.
  3. Click Convert — The tool maps your percentage to the corresponding letter grade and GPA value instantly.

Understanding Your Results

The most widely used US grading scale assigns A (4.0) to scores 90–100%, B (3.0) to 80–89%, C (2.0) to 70–79%, D (1.0) to 60–69%, and F (0.0) below 60%. Some schools use plus/minus modifiers (e.g., B+ = 3.3, B− = 2.7). GPA is calculated by averaging the grade points across all courses, weighted by credit hours. Honor roll typically requires a 3.5+ GPA and academic probation often triggers below a 2.0. Conversion results are approximations — your institution's official scale is authoritative.

Example Calculation

A student earned 88% in Biology (3 credits) and 74% in History (3 credits). Biology converts to a B (3.0 GPA points) and History converts to a C (2.0 GPA points). Semester GPA = (3.0 × 3 + 2.0 × 3) ÷ (3 + 3) = 15 ÷ 6 = 2.5. That is a solid C+ average for the term. If the student wants a 3.0 cumulative GPA after completing a third 4-credit course, they can calculate the minimum required grade using the weighted GPA formula to set a realistic study target.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does an 89% sometimes become a B+ and sometimes just a B?

Whether plus/minus grades are used depends on the institution. Schools using a straight letter scale round 80–89% to B regardless of the decimal. Schools using plus/minus modifiers typically assign B+ to 87–89% and B to 83–86%. Always check your course syllabus or registrar's stated scale to know which system applies to your grade.

How do international grading scales differ from the US 4.0 GPA system?

Many countries use percentage or 10-point scales. The UK uses First (70%+), Upper Second, Lower Second, and Third class. Germany uses 1–5 where 1 is best. When applying internationally, use the official converter provided by the receiving institution or a credential evaluation service like WES for accurate equivalency.

Does converting my grade affect my official transcript?

No. A grade converter is a planning and reference tool only. Your official transcript reflects exactly what your institution recorded. If you believe a grade is incorrect, contact your instructor or registrar directly. Transcript amendments require formal institutional processes — no external calculator affects your official academic record.

What is the minimum GPA required for most graduate school programs?

Most graduate programs set a minimum GPA of 3.0 (B average) for admission consideration. Highly competitive programs such as medical school, law school, or top MBA programs typically expect 3.5 or higher. Some programs accept students conditionally below 3.0 if other factors like GRE scores, work experience, or research background are strong.

Can I raise a low GPA by retaking courses?

Yes, but GPA improvement takes time because each new course is weighted equally with past ones. Earning an A in a 3-credit course adds 12 GPA points. Grade replacement policies vary — some schools average repeated courses, others replace the original grade entirely. Check your academic catalog and use a GPA calculator to model realistic recovery scenarios before registering for retakes.
