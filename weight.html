Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between pounds and ounces?

One pound equals sixteen ounces. Ounces are more useful for smaller food, ingredient, and parcel measurements.

Why do many fitness plans use kilograms?

Kilograms are standard in science, medicine, and most international health references, so they travel better across systems.

Can I use this for shipping?

Yes, but carriers often round up by ounce or pound for billing. Convert first, then confirm the carrier rule.

Why include stones?

Stone is still widely used in the UK and Ireland for body weight, so it helps when reading international health content.

Should I round grams in recipes or supplements?

Only lightly. Small gram differences can matter in baking, coffee, nutrition, and lab work.

Does the same conversion apply to body weight and package weight?

Yes. The math is identical. Only the rounding standard changes depending on the context.
