Frequently Asked Questions

What mistake shows up most in length conversion?

People mix feet and inches constantly. Twelve inches make one foot, and tiny errors can snowball on cuts, shipping sizes, and room plans.

When should I use meters instead of feet?

Meters are standard in most countries and in technical documents. Feet and inches are still common in U.S. construction and consumer sizing.

Why show all output units at once?

It saves time. You can compare specs, packaging sizes, and plan dimensions without rerunning the same number again and again.

Can I use this for flooring, lumber, or fabric?

Yes for the unit conversion itself. Just add waste allowance, seam allowance, or cut loss after the conversion.

How much precision do I need?

For everyday projects, a few decimals are enough. For engineering or machining, match the tolerance required by the job.

Does this help with international product listings?

Yes. It is useful when a marketplace expects metric dimensions while your source data is in imperial, or the other way around.
