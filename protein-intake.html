📋 What to Do Next:

  1. Track your intake: Use a nutrition app or food journal for 3-5 days to see your current protein consumption
  2. Plan meals: Distribute daily protein across 3-4 meals (distribute rather than front-load)
  3. Choose quality sources: Mix lean meats, fish, eggs, dairy, legumes, nuts, and plant-based options
  4. Monitor progress: After 4 weeks, assess energy levels, muscle soreness, and body composition changes
  5. Adjust as needed: If not seeing results, increase intake by 5-10% or modify activity level

Frequently Asked Questions

How much protein does my body actually need?

The Recommended Dietary Allowance (RDA) is 0.8g per kg for sedentary adults. However, active individuals need more: 1.2-1.6g/kg for general fitness, 1.6-2.2g/kg for muscle building. Your calculator considers activity level and goals. Higher protein supports muscle recovery, increases satiety (feeling full), and helps preserve muscle during weight loss.

Can too much protein damage my kidneys?

For healthy individuals, high protein intake does not damage kidneys. Research shows people consuming 2-3g/kg daily for years experience no kidney issues. However, those with pre-existing kidney disease must restrict protein. If you have kidney concerns, consult a physician before increasing protein. Stay well-hydrated (2-3L water daily) when consuming high protein.

What's the best timing for protein consumption?

Total daily intake matters most. However, spreading protein across meals (25-35g per meal) optimizes muscle protein synthesis better than consuming it all at once. Post-workout is beneficial (within 2 hours) for muscle recovery. Consuming protein with breakfast also improves satiety. For weight loss, higher protein at each meal helps reduce appetite and cravings.

Are plant-based proteins as good as animal proteins?

Animal proteins (meat, fish, eggs, dairy) contain all 9 essential amino acids in optimal ratios. Most plant proteins lack one or more amino acids. However, combining different plant sources (rice + beans, hummus + pita) creates complete proteins. Vegans/vegetarians can meet protein needs by varying sources and may need 10% more total protein due to lower bioavailability.

Should I use protein powder supplements?

Protein powder is convenient, not necessary. Whole foods provide additional nutrients (vitamins, minerals, fiber). Powder works best as supplementary when whole-food intake is insufficient. Quality matters: choose whey (complete, fast-absorbing), casein (slow), plant-based blends, or collagen. Check labels for added sugars. Cost-effective: ~£0.50/20g protein vs. £2.50 for chicken breast.

Why does my protein need increase with activity?

Exercise causes muscle protein breakdown. Higher protein intake provides amino acids to repair and build muscle. During intense training, muscle turnover increases 2-3x. Inadequate protein during heavy exercise prevents adaptation, reduces strength gains, and increases recovery time. This calculator accounts for activity to ensure you consume enough protein for your training stimulus.
