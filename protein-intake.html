What to do next

  1. Use the target as a daily average, not a perfection contest.
  2. Spread protein across meals if that helps adherence.
  3. Pair intake with resistance training for best results.
  4. Adjust for digestion, appetite, and medical needs.
  5. Talk with a dietitian or clinician if needed.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much protein do lifters need?

Needs vary, but active people and strength trainees often aim higher than the general minimum.

Is more protein always better?

Not necessarily. There is a point of diminishing return.

Why use body weight?

It is a simple practical method for setting a target.

Should I divide protein across meals?

Many people find that easier for appetite and digestion.

Can I use goal body weight instead?

Sometimes, especially in higher body fat situations, but context matters.

Does age affect protein needs?

It can. Older adults may benefit from paying closer attention to intake.
