What to do next

  1. Use zones as a general guide during cardio.
  2. If you feel unwell, stop and seek medical guidance.
  3. Do not use this as a hard medical threshold.
  4. Consider perceived effort along with heart rate.
  5. Re-check if training goals change.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is max heart rate exact from age?

No. Age-based formulas are estimates and individual response varies a lot.

Why use training zones?

They help structure cardio intensity.

Can medications affect heart rate?

Yes. That is one reason formulas are not perfect.

Should beginners train at max heart rate?

Usually no. Most training happens well below max.

Is this safe for everyone?

Anyone with health concerns should follow medical guidance before intense exercise.

Can fitness level change max heart rate?

It can affect performance more than it affects the age-based estimate.
