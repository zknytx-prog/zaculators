What This Calculator Estimates

This property tax page estimates annual property tax on a home after exemptions and shows the approximate monthly impact. It is useful when you are comparing neighborhoods, checking affordability, or building a homeowner budget.

How to Use This Calculator

  1. Enter the assessed property value from the local tax authority.
  2. Enter the annual property tax rate as a percent.
  3. Enter any exemption amount that lowers the taxable value.
  4. Submit the form to view annual tax and monthly equivalent.
  5. Recalculate with different assumptions to compare properties or exemption changes.

Formula / Method Used

  • Taxable value = Assessed property value - Exemptions
  • Annual property tax = Taxable value x Tax rate
  • Monthly estimate = Annual property tax / 12

The calculator does not allow taxable value to go below zero.

Worked Example

With an assessed value of $325,000, a rate of 1.25%, and exemptions of $25,000, the taxable value is $300,000. Estimated annual property tax is $3,750, or about $312.50 each month.

What the Result Means

The top number is the annual tax estimate based on the inputs you entered. The supporting line shows the taxable value and the monthly equivalent, which can help when reviewing escrow or housing budgets.

Common Mistakes

  • Entering a listing price instead of the assessed value used for tax billing.
  • Ignoring local reassessment caps or limits that affect the final bill.
  • Forgetting parcel fees, district taxes, or supplemental assessments.
  • Using a stale rate from a previous tax year.

Official References

Frequently Asked Questions

What does this property tax calculator estimate?

It estimates annual property tax by subtracting exemptions from assessed value, applying the tax rate, and then showing a monthly equivalent.

Can I use market value instead of assessed value?

You should usually use the assessed value from the taxing authority because that is the figure used for billing in many locations.

Does this include special assessments?

No. Special district charges, parcel fees, and supplemental assessments are not automatically included.

Why does the monthly number matter?

The monthly equivalent can help when budgeting for an escrow payment or comparing the carrying cost of different homes.

Where should I verify the actual tax bill?

Verify current rates with the official government source for your city, county, municipality, or tax assessor.

General Disclaimer

This calculator provides property tax estimates only and is not tax, legal, real-estate, or lending advice. Official bills can include additional charges and location-specific rules not modeled here.
