Frequently Asked Questions

What is payback period?

It is the amount of time needed for cumulative cash inflows to recover the original investment.

Why do businesses use payback period?

It gives a quick view of how long capital is tied up before the project earns back its cost.

Does a short payback mean the project is best?

Not always. A project can pay back quickly and still have a lower total return than another option.

Does this include discounting?

No. For time-value-of-money analysis, use NPV or a discounted payback method instead.

What counts as annual cash inflow?

Use the net cash benefit you expect each year after operating costs tied to the project.

Can I include a residual value?

Yes. Add expected salvage or resale value to see how it shortens the recovery period.
