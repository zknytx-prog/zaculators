Frequently Asked Questions

What does PPI mean?

PPI means pixels per inch. It tells you how tightly packed the pixels are on a display.

Why does higher PPI look sharper?

More pixels packed into each inch create smoother text, finer details, and less visible jagged edges.

Is PPI the same as screen resolution?

No. Resolution is the total pixel count across width and height, while PPI combines that with the physical screen size.

What PPI is considered good?

Around 150 to 220 PPI is solid for many monitors. Phones and premium tablets often go much higher.

Does higher PPI always mean a better screen?

Not always. Color accuracy, brightness, contrast, refresh rate, and panel quality matter too.

Can I use this for print?

This page is for display density, not print DPI planning. Print work follows a related but different workflow.
