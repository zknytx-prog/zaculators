What This Calculator Estimates

This property tax calculator estimates annual property tax from three inputs: assessed value, tax rate, and exemptions. It also shows a monthly equivalent so you can budget for escrow or compare properties more easily.

How to Use This Calculator

  1. Enter the assessed property value used by the tax authority.
  2. Enter the annual property tax rate as a percentage.
  3. Enter any exemption amount that reduces taxable value.
  4. Click Estimate Tax to see the annual and monthly estimate.
  5. Change the inputs to compare different tax-rate or exemption scenarios.

Formula / Method Used

The calculator follows this method:

  • Taxable value = Assessed value - Exemptions
  • Annual property tax = Taxable value x Property tax rate
  • Monthly estimate = Annual property tax / 12

If exemptions are larger than the assessed value, taxable value is treated as zero.

Worked Example

Assume an assessed value of $325,000, a tax rate of 1.25%, and exemptions of $25,000. The taxable value is $300,000. Annual property tax is $3,750, which is about $312.50 per month.

What the Result Means

The main result is the estimated annual property tax. The detail line shows the taxable value after exemptions and the monthly equivalent. Use it as a budgeting estimate for ownership costs, not as a final tax bill.

Common Mistakes

  • Using market value instead of the assessed value used by the local tax office.
  • Entering a millage rate as a percent without converting it correctly.
  • Forgetting homestead or senior exemptions that reduce taxable value.
  • Ignoring special assessments, parcel fees, or district charges.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does this property tax calculator estimate?

It estimates annual property tax by applying the property tax rate to the taxable value after exemptions, then shows an approximate monthly equivalent.

What value should I use for assessed value?

Use the assessed value or taxable assessed value from your property notice, not necessarily the recent market sale price.

Do exemptions reduce the tax bill?

Yes. This calculator subtracts exemptions from the assessed value before applying the tax rate, which lowers the taxable amount.

Why can the official bill differ from this estimate?

Official bills can differ because local levies, special assessments, parcel fees, reassessment rules, and jurisdiction-specific discounts are not included here.

Where should I confirm the final rate?

Verify current rates with the official government source for your city, county, municipality, or tax assessor.
