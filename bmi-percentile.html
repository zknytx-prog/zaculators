What to do next

  1. Use this only as a rough screen.

  3. Track trends over time, not one number.
  4. Avoid overreacting to one isolated result.
  5. Get professional advice for real concerns.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this a real pediatric percentile chart?

No. It is only a rough reminder tool and not a substitute for proper age- and sex-based charts.

Why is child BMI different from adult BMI?

Children are interpreted using percentile curves because growth changes by age and sex.

Should I rely on this alone?

No. Use proper clinical growth references.

Can I use it under age 2?

No. This tool is not intended for that age range.

Why include it at all?

Because parents often need a quick prompt before checking proper charts.

Should I ask a pediatrician?

Yes, especially if there are growth concerns.
