What to do next

  1. Double-check whether the distance is one-way or round trip.
  2. Add food and lodging separately if it is a longer trip.
  3. Budget a small cushion above the estimate for price changes.
  4. Compare your trip cost with flights or rental options.
  5. Use real-world MPG from recent driving, not optimistic brochure numbers.

Frequently Asked Questions

Should I include the return trip?

Yes. Enter total round-trip miles if you want the full cost.

Are tolls and parking required?

No. They are optional extras, but including them makes the estimate more honest.

Why is my real cost higher than this?

Traffic, detours, higher speeds, and lower real-world MPG can all raise cost.

Can I use this for delivery work?

Yes. It is useful for route planning and quick expense estimates.

Should I round fuel price up?

Usually yes, especially if prices are moving up in the region.

Does weather matter on long trips?

Yes. Wind, cold weather, and mountains can lower fuel economy.
