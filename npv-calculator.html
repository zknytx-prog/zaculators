Frequently Asked Questions

What is NPV?

Net present value compares the present value of future cash inflows to the upfront investment cost.

Why is discount rate important?

It reflects required return, risk, or the opportunity cost of tying up money in the project.

What does a positive NPV mean?

It suggests the project is expected to create value above the required return built into the discount rate.

Can I use uneven yearly cash flows here?

Not on this version. It assumes equal annual cash flow for speed and simplicity.

What is profitability index?

It is the ratio of present value of inflows to the initial investment. Above 1.0 generally means value creation.

Is NPV better than payback period?

For many capital decisions, yes, because NPV accounts for both time value of money and total project value.
