Closing Costs Calculator

Zaculators / Real Estate

What This Calculator Estimates

This calculator estimates a rough closing-cost total by applying your closing-cost percentage to the purchase price, then adding any prepaid taxes or insurance you expect to bring to closing. It is useful for building a cash-to-close plan before you receive lender paperwork.

Formula / Method Used

The estimate uses two pieces: base closing costs equal purchase price multiplied by the closing-cost percentage, and total closing costs equal base closing costs plus prepaid items. This gives a simple planning estimate rather than a document-level settlement statement.

Worked Example

If a home costs $350,000, estimated closing costs are 3%, and prepaid items are $2,500, the base fee estimate is $10,500. Adding prepaid items produces a total estimated closing cost of $13,000.

How to Interpret the Result

Use the result as a budgeting number for cash needed at closing. A higher percentage or larger prepaid amount means you may need more funds than just your down payment, even if the home price itself is unchanged.

Common Mistakes

  • Assuming every market uses the same closing-cost percentage.
  • Leaving out prepaid insurance, escrow deposits, or taxes.
  • Confusing down payment funds with closing costs.
  • Relying on an estimate after lender fees or credits have changed.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does this closing costs calculator estimate?
It estimates total closing costs by combining a percentage-based closing cost estimate with the prepaid taxes or insurance amount you enter.

Why are actual closing costs often different?
Actual closing costs depend on lender fees, title charges, transfer taxes, escrow requirements, prepaid items, and local rules that are not identical in every transaction.

Should prepaid taxes and insurance be included?
Yes. If you expect prepaid property taxes, homeowners insurance, or escrow funding at closing, adding them gives a more realistic cash-to-close estimate.

Does this calculator replace a Loan Estimate or Closing Disclosure?
No. It is only a planning tool and does not replace the formal documents provided by your lender and settlement team.

When should I update the estimate?
Update it whenever the purchase price, estimated fee percentage, lender choice, or prepaid items change.

This calculator provides real-estate planning estimates only. Actual closing costs can vary based on lender charges, title work, transfer taxes, prepaid escrows, and negotiated credits. Results are estimates only.
