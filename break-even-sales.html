What This Calculator Estimates

This calculator estimates the number of units and revenue needed to break even based on your current assumptions.

How to Use This Calculator

  1. Enter fixed costs, selling price, and variable cost per unit.
  2. Make sure all values use the same time period.
  3. Click Calculate Break-Even to view the estimate.
  4. Review the unit target and revenue estimate shown.
  5. Test different assumptions as pricing or costs change.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is break-even sales?
Break-even sales is the point where contribution from sales covers fixed costs without an estimated profit or loss.

What is contribution margin?
Contribution margin is selling price per unit minus variable cost per unit.

Can this estimate help with pricing scenarios?
Yes. Changing price or variable cost can show how your break-even point might move.

Does this include taxes or financing costs?
No. Add those into your planning separately if they matter to your scenario.

When should I update the estimate?
Update it when costs, pricing, or sales assumptions change.

This calculator provides business planning estimates only. Actual costs, pricing, margins, and sales performance may vary from simplified assumptions.
