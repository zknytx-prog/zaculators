Frequently Asked Questions

What is future value?

Future value is the amount a present sum may grow to after compounding at a chosen rate over time.

Why does compounding frequency matter?

More frequent compounding generally increases the ending value because growth is added more often.

Can I use zero growth?

This page expects a non-negative rate. At zero, the future value simply stays equal to the starting amount.

How is this different from an annuity calculator?

Future value here starts with one lump sum, while an annuity calculator models repeated contributions.

Does this account for inflation?

No. It shows nominal growth. Use the inflation calculator to think about real purchasing power.

Is this guaranteed investment performance?

No. It is a projection based entirely on the assumptions you enter.
