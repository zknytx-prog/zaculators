Vehicle Loan Calculator

Zaculators / Automotive

What This Calculator Estimates

This calculator estimates a monthly vehicle loan payment from the vehicle price, down payment, interest rate, and term length. It also shows the total paid across the loan term for planning purposes.

Formula / Method Used

  1. Subtract the down payment from the vehicle price to get the financed amount.
  2. Convert the annual interest rate into a monthly rate.
  3. Apply the standard amortized loan-payment formula across the number of months entered.
  4. Multiply the monthly payment by the term to estimate total paid.

Worked Example

If the vehicle price is $28,000, the down payment is $4,000, the rate is 6.5%, and the term is 60 months, the calculator estimates the monthly payment on the remaining $24,000 financed amount.

Common Mistakes

  1. Forgetting to include taxes or dealer fees in the purchase amount you want to finance.
  2. Using the teaser rate instead of the approved lender rate.
  3. Comparing payment size without looking at total paid over time.
  4. Ignoring depreciation and possible negative equity risk.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does this vehicle loan calculator work?

It subtracts the down payment from the vehicle price, then uses a standard fixed-rate amortization formula to estimate the monthly payment.

Does this include taxes and dealer fees?

No. This page uses the vehicle price you enter and does not automatically add registration, sales tax, warranties, or dealer add-ons.

What happens if I enter a zero interest rate?

The calculator divides the financed amount evenly across the loan term when the interest rate is zero.

Why might my lender quote a different payment?

Lenders may include taxes, fees, rebates, insurance products, or different compounding assumptions that are not modeled here.

Can I compare different down payments and loan terms?

Yes. Recalculate with different down payments, rates, or months to compare affordable payment ranges.

Limitations / Disclaimer

This calculator is a simplified financing estimate. It does not include taxes, registration, lender fees, optional products, changing rates, or credit-based approval terms that can affect a real loan offer.
